REGIONAL MUSEUM "CASA VERDE" OF TUXTEPEC

As a regional museum, this space aims to reflect the communities of the Cuenca region, made up of people who carry a rich legacy resulting from the syncretism of contributions from ethnic groups and cultures that have populated the area since ancient times. These reciprocal influences, mestizaje, and mixtures stem from their interactions with other cultures, as well as the significant biological and geographical diversity that defines the region.

We consider the Regional Museum of Tuxtepec to be a living museum. Beyond sharing the history of the towns and communities that form this great geographical and cultural mosaic, it is committed to showcasing the current ways of life of the region’s inhabitants, how they use natural resources, and how they interpret and represent the world around them. What we call material and spiritual culture is reflected here. A key focus of the museum’s temporary exhibits and activities will be on environmental education and the region’s natural resources. This vast natural heritage should be seen as part of the cultural heritage. Without the former, the latter would not exist. People settle, live, and thrive in specific regions for a reason.

The choice of settlement is tied to specific geographical conditions, which form part of a people's culture and shape its development. Recognizing the man-nature relationship is essential to the dialogue this space seeks to foster with its visitors. We might say this is a human geography project where cultural forms—human influence organized into societies—shape natural landscapes, creating distinct landscapes for each culture in a given time and space.
